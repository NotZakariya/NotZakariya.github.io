Quick disclaimer:  

Firstly, I've tried to use translations that balance accuracy and spirit, so obviously these aren't going to be 1:1. If you have any better translations, let me know and I'll consider using them.

Secondly, you don't have to agree with all the quotes on this list (I don't and often nor do the people writing them). They're supposed to evoke introspection, sometimes by being completely wrong. Kierkegaard is a perfect example of this, he uses this technique a lot in his writing. What he says is rarely what he wants you to actually think. This kind of socratic questioning of our beliefs is akin to the Hegelian system of dialectics: that is to say that comparing your own thesis to an opposite antithesis is a good way to provoke constructive thought and introspection. **So... be very wary of what you read here, don't be swayed to agree with an aphorism, just because it sounds cool, like so many people do.**

> ***“To be ironic is to pretend ignorance, or to adopt a stance opposed to the truth for the sake of the truth.”***
>*~Kierkegaard*

>***"I know one thing: that I know nothing"***  
>*~Socrates*

>***"The unexamined life is not worth living"***  
>*~Socrates*

>***"Know thyself (γνῶθι σεαυτόν)"***  
>*~Delphic Maxims*

>***"Wealth consists not in having great possessions, but in having few wants."***  
>*~Epictetus*

>***“Out of the crooked timber of humanity, nothing entirely straight can be fashioned."***  
>*~Kant*

>***“Two things will fill the mind with ever increasing wonder and awe, the more often and the more intensely the mind of thought is drawn to them, the starry heavens above me and the moral law within me."***  
>*~Kant*

>***“Man is by nature a political animal."***  
>*~Aristotle*

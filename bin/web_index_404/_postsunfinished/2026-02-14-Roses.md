Supposedly, today is "Valentine's Day" - the day that our society has dedicated to romantic love. And yet ironically enough, I don't think I have thought less about love than today. The thought of love, my love, has not so much as nibbled at the edges of my mind. It has left me completely untouched... completely free...

I can only describe this state... as pure peace and bliss. 

I can only describe it as... perfection.

In murky waters and stormy seas, introspection with clarity is difficult. But today my mind is still, it sleeps a blissful slumber, and so today, I may gaze upon its dispositions unimpeded.

I feel no desire, no want, no lust, no pain, no fear, and yet I see that my heart still is filled with love. 

Firstly, there is divine love. For my supreme creator. I feel as a submissive slave does, trusting in his master. Whether I were to be showered with blessing or tested till the skin were flayed off my body, in this moment, I would not care. God may do with me as he pleases, if I am blessed, I shall show gratitude, if I am tested, I shall show patience, My love exists in my submission, and to God I have entrusted all my affairs, expecting nothing, fearing nothing.

Secondly, there is that filial love. For my worldly creators. Just as I love my creator dutifully, so too do I love his means. Despite the fact that they still treat me as a mere child, I feel a dutiful love, a gratitude to my parents who have been nothing but good to me. My upbringing has been untouched by harm, psychological or physical, I have been blessed with food, security, education, luxury and tolerance, and for that I am grateful too...

Thirdly, there is that general love, to my fellow man. Yesterday I walked by a guy that I had only seen in passing. I nodded to him as I went about my day and felt the awkward palpable tension of acquaintancey as we walked by each other. Today, I said hello, we chatted, exchanged names, laughed, and a few hours later, along with some other friends, we were watching a show in my room whilst eating mochi and laughing as if we'd known each other for much longer - and no, not those callous superficial laughs, those performative, theatrical facades that are thick with a forced fakeness... You see, there are 3 types of laughter around "friends". The first, is the forced, insincere kind, to maintain amicability. The second, is a laugh that releases tension. Some philosophers have argued that laughing is a mechanism by which we release our pent up emotional tension, which is why some of us laugh when we're in an awkward position, or anxious. The third, similar to the second, is a release of tension, except that the tension being released isn't the tension of social anxiety or awkwardness as a result of being around these so called "friends", the tension being released is a safe, warm tension, formed with those friends, not because of them. Anyways, I digress, the point being, that I was once again reminded that austerity often exists in being a stranger. We're all human after all, all fellow passengers to the grave, common travelers, common man...

Fourth, there is that love to one's close friends. Definitionally, according to Aristotle at least, there are 3 ways of talking about friendship.
Firstly, there are friends of utility. People that one spends time with because of functional social utility, like colleagues, team members, room mates.

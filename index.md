# Start:
<div dir="rtl" lang="ar">

  أعوذُ باللهِ منَ الشيطانِ الرجيمِ،
<br>
  بسمِ اللهِ الرحمنِ الرحيمِ
</div>
Starting any piece of writing is incredibly difficult... Just like how every raindrop is more noticeable when it starts to rain, every word feels more weighty when you start to write. And when words feel more weighty, there's a natural disposition to be particular and precise to a fault - to overthink, which often leads to cryptic austerity.

So how do you write an introduction? I've tried analogies, thesis statements, quotes, prose - and yet every time, I feel dissatisfied. Every introduction that I craft feels insufficient. Maybe it's because I'm not that well-written, maybe it's because of the ontological insufficiency of human writing, I don't know...

But I have come to realise that there's a right way to start a piece of writing, or anything worth doing for that matter - to begin in the name of God.

<p dir="rtl" lang="ar">بسمِ اللهِ الرحمنِ الرحيمِ</p>

I am liable to defects and my words might be tainted with imperfection, but God is free from faults.
